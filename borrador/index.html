🔗 Project Transition Paragraph
From Single-Purpose Utility to Modular Infrastructure
Following the successful deployment of the Mirgor Monitoring System (MMS), it became clear that the QA department required an even more versatile tool—one that could break free from single-vehicle constraints and match the heavy capabilities of commercial diagnostic equipment. Taking the telemetry insights from MMS and cross-referencing them with the multi-protocol needs of standard CANoa systems, I launched a 1.5-year development lifecycle to build Crucero. While MMS proved that custom validation hardware could successfully replace manual lab testing, Crucero took that philosophy to the next level by turning a rigid validation tool into a highly scalable, modular, and software-decoupled test infrastructure.
